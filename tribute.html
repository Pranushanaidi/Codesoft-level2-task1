KIRAN BEDI

Kiran Bedi is a renowned Indian social activist and retired police officer. She was the first woman to join the Indian Police Service (IPS) in 1972 and has since become a symbol of women's empowerment and social reform in India.

"I joined the police force because I wanted to be a voice for the voiceless."

Key Achievements

  • Served as Inspector General of Prisons in Tihar Jail (1993-1995)
  • Received the Ramon Magsaysay Award for Government Service (1994)
  • Founder of two NGOs: Navjyoti and India Vision Foundation
  • Lieutenant Governor of Puducherry (2016-2021)
